**Grey Squirrel Removal & Control Services**

Grey squirrels are incredibly persistent pests that frequently invade attics, crawlspaces, and wall voids. While they may seem harmless outdoors, these agile rodents can cause severe structural damage and create fire hazards once inside a building. Our professional grey squirrel control services focus on safe removal, damage repair, and long-term prevention.

**Grey Squirrel Habits & Behavior**

Grey squirrels are highly active, territorial rodents known for their exceptional climbing and chewing abilities. They live in nests called dreys in trees but will readily seek out warm home attics for nesting, especially during breeding seasons. They are creatures of habit and will aggressively try to re-enter a preferred nesting site if they are blocked out without professional exclusion.

**When Grey Squirrels Are Active**

**Daily**

* Most active during early morning and late afternoon (diurnal)

**Seasonal**

* Spring & Summer: High activity (breeding, nesting, and foraging)
* Fall: Heavy caching behavior (storing nuts and acorns)
* Winter: Active daily but spend more time inside warm attics

Property damage and noise inside walls peak during the spring and fall breeding seasons.

**Where Grey Squirrels Commonly Cause Damage**

Grey squirrels look for vulnerabilities high up on structures, commonly targeting:

* Attic spaces and crawlspaces
* Rooflines, fascia boards, and soffits
* Construction gaps and ridge vents
* Chimneys and exhaust vents
* Siding, gutters, and trim boards

Chewed entry holes are often accompanied by dark rub marks from the oils in their fur.

**Grey Squirrel Chewing & Structural Damage**

Grey squirrels must constantly chew to keep their teeth sharp, leading to extensive property hazards that:

* Chew through wooden support beams and roof framing
* Gnaw on electrical wiring, creating serious fire hazards
* Tear up, compress, and contaminate attic insulation
* Damage PVC pipes, leading to hidden water leaks

Their presence quickly degrades home energy efficiency and compromises structural safety.

**What Grey Squirrels Eat**

Grey squirrels are opportunistic foragers with a diet focused on high-energy foods, including:

* Nuts, acorns, and seeds
* Tree bark, buds, and young shoots
* Garden fruits and vegetables
* Birdseed from backyard feeders
