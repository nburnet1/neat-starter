**Bat Removal & Exclusion Services**

Bats are protected, highly beneficial mammals that become a serious health and structural hazard when they roost inside a home. A bat colony in an attic or walls can rapidly deposit large amounts of highly toxic waste, known as guano. Our professional bat exclusion services focus on humane removal, strict legal compliance, and thorough biohazard remediation.

**Bat Habits & Behavior**

Bats are nocturnal, flying mammals that live in colonies ranging from a few individuals to hundreds. They are highly loyal to their roosts and will return to the exact same attic year after year. Bats have very fragile skeletons and can gain entry into a home through cracks as thin as a pinky finger, using the structure for warmth and protection.

**When Bats Are Active**

**Daily**

* Active from dusk until dawn while hunting for insects

**Seasonal**

* Spring: Colonies return to maternity roosts to give birth
* Summer: High nocturnal activity while raising young
* Fall: Preparing for migration or local hibernation
* Winter: Hibernating in deep structural voids (depending on species)

State regulations strictly protect bats; legal exclusion can only be performed during specific seasonal windows when flightless pups are not present.

**Where Bats Commonly Roost**

Bats prefer high, dark, and warm areas within a building, frequently occupying:

* Attic spaces and unfinished rafters
* Wall cavities, brick voids, and behind chimneys
* Gaps behind shutters and fascia boards
* Ridge vents, gable vents, and roof flashing junctions

Roosts are often identified by dark, greasy stains around tiny exterior entry gaps.

**Bat Guano & Biohazard Damage**

The primary danger of a bat infestation is the accumulation of guano and urine, which:

* Piles up quickly, causing structural ceilings to rot or collapse from weight
* Breeds Histoplasma capsulatum, a dangerous fungus that causes severe respiratory illness (Histoplasmosis)
* Emits a pungent, ammonia-like odor that penetrates living spaces
* Introduces bat bugs, which look and behave exactly like bed bugs

Guano accumulation poses an immediate risk to the air quality and health of everyone in the home.

**What Bats Eat**

Bats are entirely insectivorous and provide excellent natural pest control outside, eating:

* Mosquitoes and gnats
* Moths and beetles
* Flies and crickets
