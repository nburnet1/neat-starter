**Skunk Removal & Control Services**

Skunks are common nuisance wildlife that create problems around homes due to their strong odor, digging behavior, and tendency to den close to buildings. When skunks move onto a property, they can damage lawns, create unsafe den sites, and pose health risks to people and pets. Our skunk control services focus on safe removal, odor risk reduction, damage repair, and long-term prevention.



**Understanding Skunks**

Skunks are slow-moving, nocturnal mammals that rely on digging and shelter rather than speed for protection. While best known for their defensive spray, skunks are generally non-aggressive and will only spray when threatened. They often return to the same den sites year after year if conditions remain favorable.



**When Skunks Are Active**

**Daily**

* Primarily nocturnal (active during nighttime)

**Seasonal**

* Spring: Breeding season and denning activity
* Summer: Raising young
* Fall: Increased feeding before winter
* Winter: Reduced activity, but not true hibernation

Skunk activity is most noticeable from early spring through fall.

**Where Skunks Commonly Den**

Skunks prefer areas that provide easy access, cover, and protection from predators, including:

Under decks, porches, and steps

* Beneath sheds, crawlspaces, and foundations
* Along woodpiles and brush piles
* Under concrete slabs and patios
* Near retaining walls and rock features

Dens are often discovered by odor, visible digging, or repeated sightings.

**Digging & Lawn Damage**

Skunks dig shallow cone-shaped holes while searching for food. These holes may appear overnight and can spread across lawns quickly. Common signs include:

* Small holes in turf
* Torn-up sod
* Increased nighttime wildlife activity

**What Skunks Eat**

Skunks are omnivores and opportunistic feeders. Their diet includes:

* Grubs and insects
* Small rodents
* Fruits and berries
* Pet food and garbage
* Bird eggs and fallen birdseed

Lawns with heavy grub activity are especially attractive to skunks.
