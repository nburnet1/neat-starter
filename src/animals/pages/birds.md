**Bird Removal & Deterrent Services**

Nuisance birds can cause extensive damage to commercial and residential properties through corrosive droppings, blocked vents, and noisy nesting habits. Species like pigeons, starlings, and geese create distinct property hazards that require specialized management. Our professional bird control services focus on humane removal, advanced deterrent installation, and clean-up.

**Bird Habits & Species Behaviors**

Different bird species present unique challenges based on their natural nesting and flocking instincts:

* **Pigeons & Starlings:** Highly social flocking birds that nest on roof ledges, beams, and commercial signage, leaving heavy waste behind.
* **Swallows & Swifts:** Known for building mud or saliva nests directly against exterior walls or inside active chimneys.
* **Woodpeckers & Nuthatches:** Drill holes into wood or stucco siding to search for insects, establish nests, or signal territories.
* **Geese:** Large, aggressive waterfowl that colonize lawns, ponds, and walkways, creating safety hazards and heavy ground pollution.

**When Birds Are Active**

**Daily**

* Highly active from sunrise to sunset (diurnal), with heavy roosting activity at dusk

**Seasonal**

* Spring: Peak nesting, egg-laying, and woodpecker drumming activity
* Summer: Fledglings leaving nests; geese molting on lawns
* Fall: Migratory shifting and winter roost selection
* Winter: Resident species (like pigeons) gather in large flocks on warm structures

Federal and state laws protect many migratory bird species, meaning nests with eggs or chicks cannot legally be disturbed until the young have fledged.

**Where Birds Commonly Cause Problems**

Birds exploit high, covered structural features to build nests and roost, targeting:

* Roof eaves, soffits, and architectural ledges
* Dryer vents, bathroom exhaust vents, and stove hoods
* Chimneys, flashing caps, and rain gutters
* Warehouses, loading docks, and under-canopy beams
* Lawns, golf courses, and retention ponds (specifically geese)

Nesting sites are easily spotted by dropping accumulations directly below the area.

**Bird Droppings & Nesting Damage**

Bird infestations can cause severe structural, cosmetic, and health issues that:

* Corrode roofing materials, paint, and machinery due to highly acidic droppings
* Clog exhaust vents with nesting debris, causing major fire and carbon monoxide hazards
* Ruin exterior wood and stucco siding via continuous woodpecker drilling
* Spread over 60 transmissible diseases, including Histoplasmosis, Cryptococcosis, and severe ectoparasites like bird mites

Accumulated bird droppings also create dangerous slip-and-fall hazards on walkways and stairs.

**What Birds Eat**

Nuisance birds vary in their diets, drawing them to properties for different reasons:

* Pigeons, starlings, and swifts eat discarded human food, grains, and seeds
* Swallows, nuthatches, and woodpeckers focus heavily on insects, flying bugs, and larvae
* Geese feed almost exclusively on turfgrass, agricultural crops, and aquatic plants
