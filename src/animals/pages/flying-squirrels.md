**Flying Squirrel Removal & Control Services**

Flying squirrels are small, social rodents that can quietly colonize an attic in large numbers. Unlike other wildlife, they rarely travel alone and can establish massive nesting colonies inside residential walls and ceilings. Our professional flying squirrel control services focus on safe colony removal, comprehensive home exclusion, and long-term prevention.

**Flying Squirrel Habits & Behavior**

Flying squirrels do not actually fly; they use a specialized membrane to glide effortlessly from trees to rooftops. They are highly social, strictly nocturnal, and known for nesting in large family groups called aggregations. Because of their tiny size, they can squeeze into incredibly small gaps, making them difficult to detect until an infestation is well-established.

**When Flying Squirrels Are Active**

**Daily**

* Highly active during late-night and early morning hours (nocturnal)

**Seasonal**

* Spring: Initial breeding and nesting cycle
* Summer: Foraging and raising young
* Fall: Heavy nesting site selection for cold weather
* Winter: Large colonies gather in attics to share body heat

Homeowners usually detect flying squirrels in the dead of night when the attic becomes noisy.

**Where Flying Squirrels Commonly Cause Damage**

Flying squirrels enter homes near the highest points of the roofline, establishing nests in:

* Deep attic insulation and wall cavities
* Soffits, fascia, and roof intersections
* Ridge vents, gable vents, and static roof vents
* Gaps around chimneys and dormer windows

Because they are so small, their entry points are frequently overlooked during casual inspections.

**Flying Squirrel Colony & Latrine Damage**

The biggest threat from flying squirrels stems from their community living habits, which:

* Create centralized "latrines" that ruin drywall, ceilings, and insulation with urine
* Cause strong, persistent odors that travel through home ventilation systems
* Destroy thermal efficiency by shredding insulation for massive nests
* Present fire hazards due to localized chewing on electrical wires

A small colony can cause extensive biohazard damage in a single season.

**What Flying Squirrels Eat**

Flying squirrels have a diverse diet that allows them to thrive in wooded residential areas, consuming:

* Acorns, hickory nuts, and tree seeds
* Wild berries, fruits, and fungi
* Birdseed, suet, and garden plants
* Insects and occasionally bird eggs
