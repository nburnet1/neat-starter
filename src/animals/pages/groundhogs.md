**Groundhog Removal & Control Services**
Groundhogs (also called woodchucks) are powerful burrowing animals that can cause serious damage to yards,
foundations, sheds, and crawlspaces. While they may look harmless, their extensive tunnel systems can undermine
structures and create long-term property problems if not addressed.
Our professional groundhog control services focus on safe removal, damage repair, and long-term prevention.

**Groundhog Habits & Behavior**
Groundhogs are solitary, ground-dwelling rodents best known for their digging ability. They live alone in large
underground burrow systems and are highly territorial. Once a groundhog establishes a den, it will continue
expanding it unless removed.
They are strong diggers, capable climbers, and surprisingly good swimmers, making DIY solutions unreliable.

**When Groundhogs Are Active**
● Daily: Active during daylight hours (diurnal)
● Seasonal:
○ Spring–Summer: Most active (feeding, digging, breeding)
○ Fall: Heavy feeding before hibernation
○ Winter: Hibernate underground
Most property damage occurs from spring through fall.

**Where Groundhogs Commonly Cause Damage**
Groundhogs choose areas that offer cover, stability, and easy digging, including:
● Under decks, porches, and sheds
● Beneath foundations and crawlspaces
● Along fence lines and retaining walls
● Under concrete slabs or sidewalks

● In gardens, lawns, and landscaped areas
Burrows are often hidden until soil collapses or damage becomes visible.

**Groundhog Tunnels & Burrowing Damage**
Groundhogs dig using powerful front claws and teeth, creating complex tunnel systems that:
● Can extend 20–45 feet in length
● Often have multiple entrances
● Include nesting and escape chambers

These tunnels weaken soil, create trip hazards, and can compromise foundations and structures.

**What Groundhogs Eat**
Groundhogs are herbivores and can quickly destroy landscaping and gardens. Their diet includes:
● Garden vegetables and fruits
● Grass and clover
● Flowers and ornamental plants
● Tree shoots and bark (occasionally)
