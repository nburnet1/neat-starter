**Mice Removal & Control Services**

Mice may be small, but they are among the most common and persistent indoor pests, capable of causing widespread contamination and property damage. Due to their rapid breeding cycle, a few mice can quickly turn into a massive indoor population if left unaddressed. Our professional mice control services focus on fast eradication, total structure sealing, and thorough sanitation.

**Mice Habits & Behavior**

Mice are curious, highly adaptive rodents that live in family groups. They are exceptional climbers, capable of scaling vertical walls and wire lines to find a way inside. A mouse can squeeze through an opening as tiny as a dime, allowing them to easily access pantries, kitchens, and wall voids in search of food and nesting material.

**When Mice Are Active**

**Daily**

* Primarily active at night, away from human sight (nocturnal)

**Seasonal**

* Year-Round: Breed continuously indoors if food is available
* Fall: Peak indoor migration as outdoor temperatures drop
* Winter: High indoor nesting and foraging activity

Most homeowners notice mouse problems in the autumn when mice seek out warm winter shelter.

**Where Mice Commonly Cause Damage**

Mice establish nests near consistent food sources, commonly taking up residence in:

* Kitchen cabinets, pantries, and food storage areas
* Behind baseboards, drywall, and inside wall insulation
* Utility closets, boiler rooms, and basements
* Attics and drop ceilings
* Drawers, storage boxes, and upholstered furniture

Their presence is easily identified by tiny, pointed droppings scattered along countertops and baseboards.

**Mice Contamination & Property Damage**

Mice cause continuous damage through their chewing habits and constant waste production, which:

* Spoils pantry food supplies through direct contact with feces, urine, and hair
* Destroys fiberglass and cellulose insulation to build soft nesting pockets
* Chews through soft wires behind appliances, causing hidden electrical shorts
* Spreads harmful bacteria and viruses like Hantavirus and Salmonella via droppings

Because they dribble urine constantly as they walk, they quickly contaminate wide areas of a home.

**What Mice Eat**

Mice prefer high-carbohydrate foods but will eat almost anything available to survive, including:

* Grains, cereals, oats, and baking goods
* Pet food, birdseed, and grass seeds
* Chocolate, dried fruits, and nuts
* Cardboard, paper, and glue (when foraging for nesting materials)
