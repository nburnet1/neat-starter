**Raccoon Removal & Control Services**

Raccoons are incredibly strong, intelligent, and destructive animals when they choose to nest inside a home. They can easily rip apart shingles, vents, and drywall to force their way into attics and crawlspaces. Our professional raccoon control services focus on safe animal removal, emergency damage repair, and long-term entry prevention.

**Raccoon Habits & Behavior**

Raccoons are large, nocturnal omnivores equipped with dexterous paws that act like human hands. They are capable of opening latches, tearing open roof vents, and manipulating structures to find shelter. Females are highly protective and aggressively seek out warm attic spaces during the spring to give birth and raise their litters.

**When Raccoons Are Active**

**Daily**

* Highly active at night (nocturnal)

**Seasonal**

* Spring: Peak activity for breeding and nesting in attics
* Summer: Foraging broadly with young offspring
* Fall: Heavy feeding to build up winter fat reserves
* Winter: Less active, spending long periods inside warm dens

Property damage spikes dramatically in early spring when pregnant females search for nesting sites.

**Where Raccoons Commonly Cause Damage**

Raccoons use their immense strength to breach homes at various points, including:

* Roof vents, static vents, and soffits
* Attic spaces and crawlspaces
* Chimneys (a favorite spot for nesting females)
* Underneath decks, porches, and sheds
* Spaces behind fascia boards and roof intersections

Breach points are usually highly visible due to ripped wood, bent metal, or torn shingles.

**Raccoon Destruction & Contamination Damage**

Raccoons cause extensive physical and biological damage that:

* Tears apart roof shingles, plywood, and insulation to create large entry holes
* Destroys HVAC ductwork, causing heating and cooling system failure
* Contaminates large areas with heavy piles of feces and urine (raccoons use specific "latrine" areas)
* Uncovers and chews through heavy electrical lines, presenting severe fire risks

Raccoon roundworm (Baylisascaris procyonis) found in their waste poses a severe health threat to humans and pets.

**What Raccoons Eat**

Raccoons are opportunistic feeders that will eat almost anything available, including:

* Trash and food scraps from unsecured garbage cans
* Pet food left outdoors and fallen birdseed
* Garden vegetables, sweet corn, and fallen fruit
* Grubs, frogs, fish, and small rodents
