**Rat Removal & Control Services**

Rats are highly destructive, adaptive pests that present significant health risks and structural hazards when they invade a property. Because they multiply rapidly, a minor rat presence can escalate into a major infestation in a very short time. Our professional rat control services focus on rapid trapping, complete structural exclusion, and comprehensive sanitization.

**Rat Habits & Behavior**

Rats are incredibly smart, cautious, and social rodents that live in colonies. They are excellent climbers, jumpers, and swimmers, capable of squeezing through gaps as small as a quarter. Rats are neophobic, meaning they are deeply suspicious of new objects, which makes DIY trapping methods highly ineffective against an established colony.

**When Rats Are Active**

**Daily**

* Highly active during dark, quiet night hours (nocturnal)

**Seasonal**

* Spring & Summer: High reproductive rate and outdoor foraging
* Fall: Strong migration toward indoor heating and food sources
* Winter: Heavily nested indoors, relying on human structures to survive

Rat activity becomes much more obvious in the fall as drops in temperature drive them inside.

**Where Rats Commonly Cause Damage**

Rats move through hidden pathways within a structure, frequently colonizing:

* Attics, crawlspaces, and basement areas
* Wall cavities and dropped ceilings
* Gaps behind kitchen cabinetry and large appliances
* Sewer lines, floor drains, and utility pipe chases
* Insulation layers beneath floors and roofs

Infestations are often flagged by dark grease marks left along walls from their oily fur.

**Rat Chewing & Health Hazards**

Rats have incredibly strong teeth that grow continuously, driving them to chew through materials in ways that:

* Gnaw directly on electrical wiring, creating substantial fire risks
* Chew through PEX, PVC, or lead water pipes, causing catastrophic hidden water damage
* Destroy attic and crawlspace insulation with urine, feces, and nesting tunnels
* Spread dangerous pathogens including Leptospirosis, Salmonella, and Rat-bite fever

Their constant chewing can compromise structural integrity and spoil food supplies.

**What Rats Eat**

Rats are highly opportunistic omnivores that require a daily water source and consume:

* Household food scraps and garbage
* Stored pantry goods, grains, and pet food
* Fallen birdseed, compost, and garden crops
* Meat, fish, and nuts
