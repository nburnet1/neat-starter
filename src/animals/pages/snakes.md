**Snake Removal & Control Services**

Discovering a snake on your property or inside your home can be an alarming experience. While most common property snakes are non-venomous and help control rodent populations, they can still bite when cornered, cause deep anxiety, and signal an underlying pest issue. Our professional snake control services focus on safe removal, habitat modification, and long-term entry exclusion.

**Snake Habits & Behavior**

Snakes are ectothermic (cold-blooded) reptiles that rely on their environment to regulate their body temperature. They are quiet, reclusive creatures that actively avoid human contact, but they will seek out residential properties if there is an abundance of shelter, warmth, and food (such as mice or insects). Snakes can climb surprisingly well and can slip through foundation gaps as thin as a pencil.

**When Snakes Are Active**

**Daily**

* Most active during warm daylight hours, though hot summer weather can drive them to hunt at night

**Seasonal**

* Spring: Emerge from winter shelters to bask, mate, and forage
* Summer: Highly active, often seeking cool, shaded areas during the heat of the day
* Fall: Look for deep, protected structural spaces to survive the cold
* Winter: Enter a state of prolonged winter dormancy (brumation) underground or inside foundations

Home breaches are most common in early spring and late autumn as snakes transition between seasons.

**Where Snakes Are Commonly Found**

Snakes seek out dark, damp, or sun-warmed areas that provide protection from predators, including:

* Crawlspaces, basements, and sump pump pits
* Gaps beneath concrete porches, steps, and walkways
* Tall grass, overgrown landscaping, and rock retaining walls
* Woodpiles, brush piles, and stacked construction materials
* Inside attics (typically following rodent scent trails up walls)

Finding a shed snake skin in an attic or crawlspace is a definitive sign of active snake presence.

**The Reality of Snake Presence**

Snakes generally do not cause structural damage to a home, but their presence indicates other risks:

* It strongly points to a hidden rodent or insect infestation that is serving as their food source
* Venomous species pose an immediate safety risk to children, adults, and household pets
* Their sudden presence inside living areas creates significant stress and anxiety for residents

Proper identification is critical, as handling venomous snakes requires specialized safety gear and training.

**What Snakes Eat**

Snakes are strict carnivores that play an important role in the eco-system by consuming:

* Mice, rats, voles, and chipmunks
* Frogs, toads, lizards, and large insects
* Bird eggs and young fledglings
